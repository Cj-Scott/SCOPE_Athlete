# SCOPE Athlete Public Messaging Readiness

This packet is the current source of truth for how SCOPE Athlete should be described during private alpha. It is intentionally conservative until reviewer feedback confirms the workflow resonates.

## Positioning

SCOPE Athlete is a local-first planning workspace for student-athletes and families who want a clearer way to compare schools, organize opportunity planning, track outreach, and keep next actions visible.

SCOPE stands for **School Comparison & Opportunity Planning Engine**.

## Problem Statement

Families often have to coordinate school lists, academic fit, soccer fit, public video links, coach contact notes, visits, tasks, and changing eligibility rules across disconnected tools. That can make the process feel harder than it needs to be, especially for families who are trying to help an athlete be seen by schools that fit their goals.

SCOPE Athlete exists to make that planning work easier to understand and act on.

## Primary Audience

- Student-athlete families reviewing possible college paths.
- Trusted club, coach, and content contacts who can evaluate whether the workflow is clear.
- Future advisors or reviewers who understand the real friction families face when comparing schools and managing follow-up.

## Safe Claims For Private Alpha

- SCOPE Athlete helps families organize school-fit planning in one local-first workspace.
- The alpha uses synthetic demo data for review.
- The tool supports school comparison, task tracking, outreach notes, video-link references, evidence review, and updateable catalog/rules data packs.
- The current alpha is designed for workflow validation, not broad public distribution.
- Feedback is reviewed manually before it becomes product work.

## Claims To Avoid

- Do not claim the tool guarantees attention from schools.
- Do not claim NCAA, school, cost, roster, or eligibility information is complete or always current.
- Do not imply endorsement by schools, leagues, clubs, NCAA, NWSL, or any governing body.
- Do not ask reviewers to enter real private athlete data during alpha.
- Do not present the unsigned Windows installer as a production-ready release.

## Screenshot And Demo Clip Backlog

Capture these after each alpha build has passed smoke review:

- First-run onboarding prompt with zero profiles.
- Overview dashboard using synthetic demo data.
- Player Profile page with profile switching.
- School Pipeline comparison with evidence card visible.
- Settings / About showing app and data-pack changelogs.
- Feedback path from the public alpha site.

Use synthetic demo data only. Do not include real student-athlete information, private outreach notes, or private reviewer responses.

## Draft Outreach Copy

Short version:

> I am testing SCOPE Athlete, a private-alpha planning workspace for student-athlete families. It is meant to make school comparison, opportunity planning, outreach notes, public video links, and next actions easier to organize. I would value your feedback on whether the workflow feels useful, clear, and trustworthy.

Reviewer ask:

> Please use the synthetic demo data, follow one walkthrough, and submit feedback through the linked form. I am looking for workflow clarity and trust concerns before making any broader public claims.

## Review Gate

Do not broaden public messaging until private alpha feedback answers these questions:

- Do reviewers understand what SCOPE Athlete is for within the first screen?
- Do reviewers trust the distinction between sourced data, estimates, gaps, and user-entered notes?
- Do families understand the next action after each workflow?
- Do reviewers see this as a planning tool rather than a promise of school attention?
