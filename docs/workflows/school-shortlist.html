Workflow B / School shortlist

Can reviewers trust the school recommendation path?

Use this script to test whether a family can compare schools, understand evidence, and leave with a confident next action.

25 minutes Scenario: shortlist decision Pipeline + Intelligence + Evidence

Scenario

A family is narrowing a school list.

They need to compare academic fit, soccer fit, division context, source confidence, and missing information without over-trusting a recommendation.

Experience goal

The app should feel like a decision support tool.

Reviewers should see why a school is recommended, what evidence supports it, what is missing, and what practical next step belongs in the pipeline.

Feature touchpoints

  • Player selector and school context
  • Top recommendations and sorting
  • School detail/intelligence view
  • Evidence/source confidence
  • Tasks and manual-review gaps

Suggested demo profile

Start with Maya Brooks. Use Nia Rivers for the rules-heavy contrast.

Maya Brooks is the best primary profile for shortlist review because she has multiple schools across fit levels, visible source notes, and a clear regional school-comparison path.

Nia Rivers is useful if you want to stress-test application timing, DII planning, and rules-generated task context after completing the main shortlist pass.

Walkthrough script

Review one shortlist decision.

  1. 01
    Load demo data and choose Maya Brooks.

    Start with the same demo records so feedback is comparable, then notice whether Maya's shortlist-building stage is clear.

  2. 02
    Open School Pipeline.

    Look at the top three recommendations and whether the cards tell a scannable story.

  3. 03
    Open one school detail view.

    Use the recommended action or detail button for one school that catches your eye.

  4. 04
    Review academics and soccer fit.

    Check whether fit score, academics, program notes, position pathway, and stage of interest feel connected.

  5. 05
    Review evidence and gaps.

    Decide whether source confidence, missing fields, and manual-review labels make the recommendation trustworthy.

  6. 06
    Try sorting or filtering.

    Confirm the pipeline responds predictably and preserves comparison context.

  7. 07
    Choose the next action.

    Decide whether the app makes it obvious to research, contact, move status, add a task, or remove the school.

Quick check

  • Fit clarity

    Do the school fit reasons make sense?

  • Evidence clarity

    Can you tell what is sourced, missing, or estimated?

  • Action clarity

    Do you know what to do after reviewing the school?

  • Decision confidence

    Would you trust this enough to discuss it with a family?

Feedback prompts

Answer these after the walkthrough.

  • Did the recommendation feel explainable?

    Note the specific score, label, or evidence that helped or hurt trust.

  • Were gaps obvious?

    Call out missing data, unclear source confidence, or school fields that need manual support.

  • Could you compare options quickly?

    Describe whether the top-three recommendation limit helped or hid useful context.

  • Did it feel like a complete decision journey?

    Note whether the path from recommendation to evidence to action felt connected.

  • What would make school review safer?

    Suggest wording, warnings, or source details that would prevent over-trusting weak data.

If you get stuck

Click-by-click fallback path.

  1. Load demo data.

    Go to Settings, select Load demo data, and confirm the demo-load warning.

  2. Choose a player.

    Use the top-left player selector and choose Maya Brooks. Use Nia Rivers only if you want the rules-heavy contrast path.

  3. Open School Pipeline.

    Select Pipeline or School Pipeline from the left navigation.

  4. Review recommendations.

    Look for the top three recommendations and read one card from top to bottom before opening details.

  5. Open school details.

    Select the school card's detail, intelligence, or review action to open the school intelligence/detail view.

  6. Check evidence.

    Find academics, soccer program, position pathway, source confidence, evidence/source notes, and manual-review gaps.

  7. Choose an action.

    Return to the pipeline or tasks area and decide whether the next step is research, outreach, status change, or task creation.

In feedback, call out whether the app itself made this path obvious or whether these instructions were necessary.
